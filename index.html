About MicroPrepared

Preparedness Shouldn't Be Overwhelming

Martin Fernway

Founder, MicroPrepared

I grew up devouring survival literature — the SAS Survival Handbook, army field manuals, anything I could get my hands on. That world always made sense to me.

I have ADHD, and over the years that taught me something useful: how to cut through dense material fast and pull out only what matters. I spent a career doing exactly that in other fields — extracting the essentials from complex resources and making them actionable.

With the world feeling less stable by the day, I came back to survival. I collected 270+ handbooks, army manuals, government guides, and training courses, then distilled them into clear, scannable, printable guides. One page. Just the essentials. Because in an emergency, focus is everything — and that's something my ADHD brain has been training for all along.

Frequently Asked Questions

What format are the guides in?

All guides are delivered as beautifully formatted PDFs that you can read on any device, print out, or store offline. You get instant access after purchase.

I'm a complete beginner. Where should I start?

Start with "The 72-Hour Home Kit" — it's our most popular guide and covers the absolute basics every household needs. From there, Water Storage 101 and Food Storage Basics are natural next steps. Or grab the Complete Starter Bundle and save.

I'm already an experienced prepper. Is this for me?

Absolutely. Our guides are distilled from 270+ expert sources, many of which you may not have encountered. Even experienced preppers find gaps in their plans. The advanced guides on communications, home defense, and long-term food preservation go well beyond beginner territory.

How are these different from free content online?

Free content is scattered, often contradictory, and buried in ads and affiliate links. Our guides are curated from verified, expert sources, organized into clear action steps, and designed to be completed in one sitting. No scrolling through forums or watching 45-minute YouTube videos for one tip.
